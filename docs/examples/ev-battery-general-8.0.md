# EV Lithium-Ion Battery Lifespan and Annual Capacity Loss

## Answer

Modern EV lithium-ion battery packs typically lose on the order of **1.8% to 2.3% of capacity per year** in real-world use, and retain enough capacity (above the common 70–80% State-of-Health end-of-life thresholds) to remain usable for roughly **10–13 years and well beyond 100,000 miles** [src-2024-battery-degradation][src-how-long-do-ev-batteries-really-last][src-ev-battery-warranty-capacity-thresholds][src-real-world-ev-study-shows-how-long-elect]. At these rates, packs are often expected to outlast the vehicles they power — the car may wear out before the battery reaches end-of-life [src-2024-battery-degradation]. Degradation rates have also been improving over time as battery chemistry and thermal management advance: the 2024 average of 1.8%/yr was about a 22% improvement over the 2019 baseline of 2.3%/yr [src-2024-battery-degradation]. Actual loss varies substantially with chemistry, cooling, climate, and charging habits — older air-cooled packs degrade roughly twice as fast as modern liquid-cooled ones [src-how-long-do-electric-car-batteries-last-2][src-how-bad-is-ev-battery-degradation-and-ho].

## Annual Capacity Loss Rates

Large-scale telematics data converge on a low-single-digit annual loss rate:

- Geotab reported a **2024 fleet average of 1.8%/yr** across roughly 5,000 EVs with about 1.5 million days of telematics data [src-2024-battery-degradation].
- A January 2026 Automotive Fleet analysis using the Geotab dataset (more than 22,700 EVs across 21 models) reported an average annual SOH loss of **~2.3%** [src-how-long-do-ev-batteries-really-last].
- Other recent analyses place the average degradation **between 1.8% and 2.3% per year**, depending on the report timeframe and dataset [src-study-never-mind-degradation-batteries-w][src-ev-battery-degradation-by-model-2026-rea].
- The 2024 rate of 1.8%/yr represented about a **22% improvement** over the 2019 baseline of 2.3%/yr, reflecting better chemistry and thermal management [src-2024-battery-degradation].
- The best-performing pack designs lose approximately **1.0%/yr** under moderate conditions [src-2024-battery-degradation].

At ~2.3%/yr, a pack reaches roughly 75% capacity after about **13 years** [src-real-world-ev-study-shows-how-long-elect]. After eight years of typical moderate usage and climate, batteries retain roughly **81.6%** of new capacity (about 88% SOH for low-DC-fast-charging vehicles, versus ~81.6% for high-utilization vehicles) [src-how-long-do-electric-car-batteries-last][src-new-geotab-data-shows-ev-battery-health].

### On the shape of the degradation curve
The findings are mixed here. Some warranty/threshold analyses describe a steeper initial "break-in" period in the first 1–2 years followed by slower decline [src-ev-battery-warranty-capacity-thresholds][src-ev-battery-warranties-2026-what-s-actual][src-nissan-leaf-battery-degradation-what-to]. However, the Geotab telematics studies do not explicitly document a faster-initial-fade-then-plateau pattern; they describe degradation as relatively consistent annually under given conditions, without clear inflection points [src-2024-battery-degradation][src-how-long-do-ev-batteries-really-last].

## Usable Lifespan, Miles, and Fleet Retirement Data

- Packs typically retain usable capacity for about **10–13 years or beyond 100,000 miles** before reaching the 70–80% SoH end-of-life thresholds [src-ev-battery-warranty-capacity-thresholds][src-real-world-ev-study-shows-how-long-elect].
- At low degradation rates, packs are expected to last **more than 20 years** with sufficient capacity for daily driving, often outlasting the vehicle itself [src-2024-battery-degradation].
- **Tesla (Model S/X/3/Y):** less than 1% pack replacement rate at ~120,000 miles across a 30,000+ vehicle global fleet; high-mileage cars retain 85–90% capacity beyond 200,000 miles [src-ev-battery-warranties-2026-what-s-actual][src-tesla-vs-ford-vs-chevy-ev-battery-longev].
- **Hyundai/Kia (2017+):** ~1–2% full pack replacement rate under 90,000 miles across 10,000+ vehicles, with many packs still above 90% capacity after ~100,000 miles [src-ev-battery-warranties-2026-what-s-actual][src-ev-battery-warranty-capacity-thresholds][src-how-long-do-electric-car-batteries-last].
- **Early Nissan Leaf (24 kWh):** notably higher replacement rates of 5–10% in early years, attributed primarily to high-temperature degradation [src-ev-battery-warranties-2026-what-s-actual].
- Modern packs exceed 75% SoH well past typical end-of-sale periods (an estimated 13+ years usable life), suggesting substantial second-life potential for grid/storage reuse — though dedicated second-life data was not covered in the available summaries [src-real-world-ev-study-shows-how-long-elect].

## Effect of Chemistry and Thermal Management

- **LFP** cathodes show good thermal stability and longer lifespan than NMC/NCA, owing to the stable FePO₄ structure; high-energy NMC/NCA chemistries degrade faster when not adequately cooled [src-a-review-of-improvements-on-electric-veh].
- After 8 years in moderate climates: **LFP packs retain ~90–93%**, while **NMC/NCA packs retain ~85–92%** [src-ev-battery-warranties-2026-what-s-actual].
- **Cooling matters strongly:** air-cooled packs (e.g., Nissan Leaf) degraded around **4.2%/yr**, versus about **2.3%/yr** for liquid-cooled systems like the Tesla Model S under similar usage [src-how-long-do-electric-car-batteries-last-2][src-how-bad-is-ev-battery-degradation-and-ho].
- Early air-cooled Leaf packs are heat-sensitive: temperatures above 86 °F accelerate fade, especially without active chilling during fast charges [src-nissan-leaf-battery-degradation-what-to]. Typical first-year loss for these packs ranged from roughly **3% to 5%**, and older 24–30 kWh units lost about **15–25% after five years** in moderate climates; later 62 kWh versions reduced subsequent annual fade [src-nissan-leaf-battery-degradation-what-to]. Thermal recalls on some 2019–2022 Leaf models limited high-temperature DC fast charging via software to mitigate degradation and safety risks [src-nissan-leaf-battery-degradation-what-to].

## Manufacturer Warranties vs. Real-World Results

- Major manufacturer warranties typically run **8 years with mileage limits of 100,000–150,000 miles** [src-tesla-vs-ford-vs-chevy-ev-battery-longev][src-ev-battery-warranties-2026-what-s-actual][src-ev-battery-warranty-capacity-thresholds].
- Ford and Chevrolet explicitly guarantee a minimum of **at least 70% capacity** over the warranty period; Tesla offers an 8-year/100,000–150,000-mile term, with real-world data indicating higher retention [src-tesla-vs-ford-vs-chevy-ev-battery-longev].
- Real-world results generally beat these floors: at ~2.3%/yr average decline, packs hold roughly **81.6% after eight years**, above the typical 70–80% warranty floors [src-new-geotab-data-shows-ev-battery-health]. Tesla packs average **1.0–2.0%/yr**, and Ford (Mustang Mach-E / F-150 Lightning) packs are reported at **1.5–2.5%/yr**, with one vehicle losing only 4% after 100,000 miles [src-tesla-vs-ford-vs-chevy-ev-battery-longev][src-new-geotab-data-shows-ev-battery-health].
- Modern packs rarely fall below 70% before warranty expiry for most chemistries [src-ev-battery-warranties-2026-what-s-actual][src-real-world-ev-study-shows-how-long-elect].

## Factors That Accelerate or Slow Degradation

- **DC fast charging (high power):** fast charging above 100 kW raises annual degradation to as much as **3.0%/yr**, versus ~1.5%/yr for AC/Level-2 or lower-power charging — roughly double [src-how-long-do-ev-batteries-really-last][src-how-bad-is-ev-battery-degradation-and-ho][src-ev-battery-degradation-by-model-2026-rea][src-geotab-study-shows-charging-power-and-fr]. Geotab identifies charging power and frequency as dominant drivers of degradation [src-geotab-study-shows-charging-power-and-fr]. Frequent DC fast charging (>12% of sessions) corresponds to ~2.3–2.5%/yr versus ~1.5%/yr for low DC fast-charge use [src-how-long-do-electric-car-batteries-last].
- **Hot climates:** add approximately **+0.4%/yr** over mild conditions [src-how-long-do-ev-batteries-really-last][src-how-long-do-electric-car-batteries-last-2][src-ev-battery-degradation-by-model-2026-rea][src-new-geotab-data-shows-ev-battery-health].
- **Cold temperatures:** cause only reversible range reduction, not permanent capacity fade, in these datasets [src-how-long-do-electric-car-batteries-last-2][src-ev-battery-degradation-by-model-2026-rea].
- **High utilization / heavy daily driving:** adds about **+0.8%/yr** relative to the lowest-use group [src-how-long-do-ev-batteries-really-last][src-study-never-mind-degradation-batteries-w][src-ev-battery-degradation-by-model-2026-rea].
- **State of charge habits:** sustained low SOC (<10%) adds roughly **+0.4%/yr**; prolonged time above 80% or storing fully charged for weeks adds about **+0.2–0.3%/yr** [src-ev-battery-degradation-by-model-2026-rea][src-how-bad-is-ev-battery-degradation-and-ho]. Occasional 100% charges are generally safe for long-term capacity retention [src-how-long-do-electric-car-batteries-last-2][src-study-never-mind-degradation-batteries-w].
- **Combined high-stress use:** high fast-charging frequency (≥50% of sessions or >3×/month) in hot climates significantly increases loss [src-how-long-do-electric-car-batteries-last-2][src-study-never-mind-degradation-batteries-w].

## Contradictions and Open Uncertainties

- **Average annual rate (1.8% vs 2.3%):** Sources report both figures, largely reflecting different dataset sizes and timeframes drawn from Geotab data (the 1.8% 2024 figure across ~5,000 EVs versus the ~2.3% figure across 22,700+ EVs) rather than a true disagreement; note these are cross-checked Geotab-derived analyses rather than fully independent datasets [src-2024-battery-degradation][src-how-long-do-ev-batteries-really-last][src-study-never-mind-degradation-batteries-w][src-ev-battery-degradation-by-model-2026-rea].
- **Shape of the curve:** Warranty-oriented sources describe an early steep "break-in" then slower decline, while the Geotab telematics studies report relatively linear annual loss without a clear documented early-fade-then-plateau pattern [src-ev-battery-warranty-capacity-thresholds][src-nissan-leaf-battery-degradation-what-to][src-2024-battery-degradation][src-how-long-do-ev-batteries-really-last].
- **Second-life applications:** Not directly covered by the available summaries; the data supports substantial second-life *potential* (packs above 75% SoH past 13+ years) but does not document actual second-life deployment outcomes [src-real-world-ev-study-shows-how-long-elect].
- **Source credibility:** Several supporting figures (chemistry-specific retention, model-specific replacement rates, warranty specifics) come from web sources with moderate credibility ratings (60–65), and some manufacturer-specific degradation figures rest partly on owner reports [src-ev-battery-warranties-2026-what-s-actual][src-tesla-vs-ford-vs-chevy-ev-battery-longev][src-new-geotab-data-shows-ev-battery-health].
