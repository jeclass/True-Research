# Prediction-markets comparison — exact prompts as sent (methodology record)

The three systems did NOT receive identical prompts for this question. Recording
verbatim what each got, because the blind scoring must account for it.

**True Research** (run `20260706-142616-022f`, fresh public clone at v1.2,
Comprehensive preset, $8 cap) received the full enumerated brief in
`prompt-prediction-markets.txt` (7 numbered sub-asks, primary-source and
honesty requirements).

**Claude (Research mode)** received:

> how to find edges in kalshi data, and the best known ways to create an auto
> trading bot on kalshi and polymarket that can find reproducible profitable
> trades. Dont ask me questions, just span out and run the deep research on
> what you think would be beneficial

**Gemini (Deep Research)** received:

> how to find edges in kalshi data, and the best known ways to create an auto
> trading bot on kalshi and polymarket that can find reproducible profitable
> trades.

**Implication for scoring:** the enumerated brief gives True Research a
structural advantage on *coverage/depth-vs-ask* (it was told which facets to
hit) and a disadvantage on *autonomy* (the hosted products had to decompose the
vague ask themselves — which is also what True Research's initializer does, but
here it was pre-decomposed by a human). Scores on **citation accuracy,
verification of load-bearing claims, and source quality are prompt-independent**
and remain the primary like-for-like axes. Depth/coverage comparisons must be
read with this asymmetry in mind, or the comparison re-run with identical
prompts for a clean coverage axis.
